  • About HCAS
    • Back
    • The Hull Children's Adventure Society bought the cottage in 1983.
    • Since then we have provided more than 1,000 holidays to families in need of a break.
    • We get annual grants from The Sir James Reckitt Charity and Headway the brain injury association which enable us to meet most of the running costs of the cottage.
    • Families reach us through social workers and teachers in Hull and through Headway groups. We offer the holidays to people who cannot usually afford to pay for a holiday because of their financial circumstances. We reserve school holiday weeks for families with school age children.
    • The cottage is looked after by the charity's trustees and volunteers. We employ a cleaner to clean the cottage between visits and to wash the bedding.
    • We welcome donations which we use to improve furniture, equipment, games and books. Please send your donation to: Kevin Curley, 6 Cumberhills Road, Duffield Belper DE56 4HA The cheque or postal order should be made payable to Hull Children's Adventure Society.

  • Disabled access
    • Back
    • Three steep steps from the front street to the front door make the cottage impossible for someone in a wheelchair to access easily
    • But if you can manage the steps we do have:
    • Ground floor double-bed-settee (Please ask Bernadette before you arrive if you need bedding for this)
    • Downstairs bathroom with shower, toilet and wash basin
    • There are also a few steps in the garden up to the play-barn and patio.
